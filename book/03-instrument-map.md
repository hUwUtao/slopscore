# Instrument Map — Ranges, Slots, Doublings

> **Ranges are practical written ranges** (conservative orchestral working ranges, not extreme limits).  
> Actual safe range depends on ensemble, player, and period.

## 1. Strings and Plucked Strings

| Instrument | Practical Written Range | Slot Profile | Timbre / Register Notes | Common Articulations & Doublings | ABC Family |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Violin I–II** | G3–A7 (no transp.) | Ld, Cm, Hy, Os, Pd, Cl | Brightest core carrier; high = lyric or brilliant lead; mid = shared melody; can play nearly any slot | leg, stac, marc, vib, port, trem, pizz, coll; doubles flute, oboe, clarinet, sometimes trumpet | STR-H |
| **Viola** | C3–E6 (no transp.) | Cm, Hy, Pd, Os, color-lead | Darker midrange; ideal for inner motion and mellow countermelody; less incisive than violin | leg, stac, marc, vib, trem, pizz, coll; doubles clarinet, English horn, bassoon, horn | STR-M |
| **Cello** | C2–C6 (no transp.) | Ld, Cm, Hy, Bs, Pd | One of the best singing low-to-mid melodic instruments; upper register = intense exposed melody | leg, marc, vib, trem, pizz, port; doubles clarinet, bass clarinet, bassoon, trombone, horn | STR-L |
| **Double Bass** | C2–C5 (sounds 8va lower) | Bs, Os, Pd, Pu | Fundamental clarifier; high register = special effect only, not default | marc, stac, leg, trem, pizz; doubles bass clarinet, bassoon, contrabassoon, tuba | BASS |
| **Harp** | C♭1–G♭7 | Hy, Os, Pd, Cl, Pu | Arpeggiated harmony, glissandi, shimmer, bell-like harmonics; poor as continuous loud lead against full orchestra | rolled chords, bisbigliando shimmer, harmonics, arpeggio gliss; pairs with winds, strings, celesta | HARP |

### Special Techniques — Strings

| Technique | Effect | Slot use |
|---|---|---|
| Col legno | Wood-on-string; percussive, between pitch and noise | Ostinato, effect |
| Sul ponticello | Near bridge; glassy, harmonics-rich | Color, effect, pad |
| Sul tasto | Over fingerboard; dark, muted | Pad, color |
| Harmonics | Bell-like, ethereal | Pad, color |
| Pizzicato | Plucked; dry, short decay | Ostinato, punctuation |
| Tremolo | Rapid bow alternation; shimmering sustain | Pad, color |

## 2. Woodwinds

| Instrument | Practical Written Range | Slot Profile | Timbre / Register Notes | Common Articulations & Doublings | ABC Family |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Piccolo** | D4–C7 (sounds 8va higher) | Ld, Cl, Pu | Piercing, brilliant; dangerous at loud dynamics; best for halo, peak, edge; avoid as primary melodic instrument in heavy textures | stac, acc, tr; doubles flute, violins, high brass peaks | WW-H |
| **Flute** | C4–D7 (written = sounding) | Ld, Cm, Os, Cl, Pd | Low register: soft and veiled; mid register: fluid and clear; high register: brilliant and penetrating; can vanish under louder textures in low range | leg, stac, acc, tr; doubles violin, oboe, clarinet | WW-H |
| **Oboe** | B♭3–A6 (written = sounding) | Ld, Cm, Pu, Cl | Highly identifiable nasal-focus timbre; ideal for exposed melody and pastoral or plaintive lines; reed is tiring — limit very long exposed phrases | leg, stac, marc, acc, vib-like shaping; doubles violin, flute, clarinet | WW-R |
| **English Horn** | B3–G6 (sounds P5 lower) | Cm, Ld, Hy, Cl | Darker and more melancholic than oboe; excellent in middle register for lyrical counterline; associated with distance, longing | leg, marc, acc; doubles viola, clarinet, horn, bassoon | WW-R |
| **Clarinet in B♭/A** | E3–C7 (B♭: sounds M2 lower; A: sounds m3 lower) | Ld, Cm, Hy, Os, Pd | Widest color span of common woodwinds; throat tones (E3–G♯3) are weak and pale; middle register rich and warm; upper register agile and brilliant | leg, stac, marc, tr; doubles violin, viola, cello, horn, bassoon | WW-C |
| **Bass Clarinet in B♭** | E♭3–G6 (sounds M9 lower, treble notation) | Cm, Hy, Bs, Pd, Cl | Bridge between clarinet warmth and bass support; very effective for dark pads and sinister doubling bass | leg, stac, marc, tr; doubles cello, bass, bassoon, trombone, horn | BASS |
| **Bassoon** | B♭1–E♭5 (written = sounding) | Cm, Hy, Bs, Os, color-lead | Dry, reedy character; can be comic or grave; tenor register (C3–C4) is very useful for singing lines; high register (above G4) = unusual exposed color | leg, stac, marc, acc, tr; doubles cello, bass clarinet, horn, low strings | WW-B |
| **Contrabassoon** | B♭1–B♭4 (sounds 8va lower) | Bs, Pd, Pu, Cl | Abyssal reinforcement; best for pedal, gravitas, and low punctuation rather than agile melodic lines | marc, stac, long sustain; doubles basses and tuba most naturally | BASS |

## 3. Brass

| Instrument | Practical Written Range | Slot Profile | Timbre / Register Notes | Common Articulations & Doublings | ABC Family |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Horn in F** | F♯2–C6 (sounds P5 lower) | Hy, Cm, Ld, Pd, Cl | Most blend-capable brass instrument; middle register: warm and noble; high register: brilliant but less soft-capable; stopped horn = special nasal color | leg, marc, stopped, acc; doubles clarinet, Eng. horn, bassoon, viola, cello, tuba at 8ve below | BR-M |
| **Trumpet** | F♯3–D6 (B♭ trumpet sounds M2 lower) | Ld, Pu, Os, Cl | Brilliant and directional; best for attacks, fanfares, bright lead, rhythmic definition; muted = buzzy or distant | marc, stac, acc, muted, lip-slur figures; doubles violin, flute/oboe, trombone at 8ve | BR-H |
| **Tenor Trombone** | E2–F5 (no transposition) | Hy, Bs, Pu, Ld | Direct, weighty, excellent for chorale and power writing; less blend than horn | marc, tenuto, leg, gliss where idiomatic; doubles cello and bassoon well | BR-L |
| **Bass Trombone** | B♭1–B♭4 (no transposition) | Bs, Ld, Pu, Os | Among the loudest low-register instruments; ideal for authority, threat, and low rhythmic declaration; williams-style themed bass line is fully possible | marc, acc, sfz, broad legato; doubles cello+bass, tuba, contrabassoon | BR-L |
| **Tuba** | D1–F4 (concert pitch) | Bs, Pd, Hy, Pu | Fundamental anchor; lyrical solo is possible but not the default; most often used for low weight and breadth | leg, marc, long sustain; doubles basses, contrabassoon, horn at 8ve | BASS |

### Special Techniques — Brass

| Technique | Effect | Slot use |
|---|---|---|
| Stopped horn | Nasal, brassy, metallic (raised by approximately a semitone) | Color, effect |
| Harmon mute (trumpet) | Buzzy, distant, Miles Davis style | Color, effect |
| Straight mute | Bright, cutting, thin | Color, punctuation |
| Cup mute | Warmer, darker version of straight mute | Color, pad |
| Flutter tongue | Rapid trill-like vibration | Effect |

## 4. Percussion and Keyboard Color

| Instrument | Practical Written Range | Slot Profile | Timbre / Register Notes | Common Articulations & Doublings | ABC Family |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Timpani** | ~D2–C4 (4–5 drums, tunable) | Bs, Pu, Os | Pitched punctuation and pedal-rhythm; can define harmony via roots/fifths; pedal glissando is possible | roll, acc, damped strokes, rhythmic ostinati; doubles bass, brass roots, low strings | TIMP |
| **Snare Drum** | unpitched | Os, Pu, Cl | Best motoric grid in the orchestra; can dominate if constant and too loud | rolls, accents, sticking patterns, caesura setup; central in *Boléro* and march writing | PERC |
| **Bass Drum** | unpitched | Pu, Cl, effect | Mass-arrival impact; easily overpowers tutti at fff | rolls, crashes, choked attacks, accented single strokes | PERC |
| **Cymbals / Tam-tam** | unpitched | Pu, Cl, effect | Cymbal: bright crash; tam-tam: dark, resonant swell that takes time to build | crash, swell, choked, bowed tam-tam | PERC |
| **Glockenspiel** | G3–C6 written (sounds +2 oct.) | Ld, Cl, Pu, Os | Articulate sparkle; projects well; poor for warm pad or sustained lines | stac, repeats, octave punctuations; doubles piccolo, celesta, violins for edge | KP |
| **Xylophone** | F3–C7 written (sounds +1 oct.) | Ld, Cl, Pu, Os | Skeletal, dry, percussive projection; bright and cutting | stac, hard-mallets; doubles glockenspiel, woodwinds at octave | KP |
| **Celesta** | C3–C7 written (sounds 8va higher) | Ld, Cl, Pd | Bell-piano hybrid; magical or icy color; usually cannot project through dense tutti | leg, detached sparkle, rolled chords; doubles harp, flutes, violins, horns in halo writing | KP |
| **Piano** | A0–C8 | Hy, Os, Pu, effect, color-lead | Percussive attack and sustain with pedal; in orchestra often used for color, articulation reinforcement, or special layer rather than as harmonic filler | marc, stac, rolled or cluster effects; doubles percussion, harp, winds | KP |
